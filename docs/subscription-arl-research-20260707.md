**CA §17603 (unconditional gift) — leginfo direct:**
> "In any case in which a business sends any goods, wares, merchandise, or products to a
> consumer, under a continuous service agreement or automatic renewal of a purchase, without
> first obtaining the consumer's affirmative consent as described in Section 17602, the goods,
> wares, merchandise, or products shall for all purposes be deemed an unconditional gift to
> the consumer, who may use or dispose of the same in any manner he or she sees fit without
> any obligation whatsoever on the consumer's part to the business, including, but not limited
> to, bearing the cost of, or responsibility for, shipping any goods, wares, merchandise, or
> products to the business."

**CA §17600 (legislative intent — opener quote):** "It is the intent of the Legislature to end
the practice of ongoing charging of consumer credit or debit cards or third party payment
accounts without the consumers' explicit consent for ongoing shipments of a product or ongoing
deliveries of service." (Stats. 2009 ch. 350 (SB 340).)

**CA §17604 (remedies + safe harbor):** "Notwithstanding Section 17534, a violation of this
article shall not be a crime. However, all available civil remedies that apply to a violation
of this article may be employed." / "If a business complies with the provisions of this
article in good faith, it shall not be subject to civil remedies."

**NY GBL §527-a(2) (gift):** "In any case in which a business sends any goods, wares,
merchandise, or products to a consumer, under a continuous service agreement or automatic
renewal of a purchase, without first obtaining the consumer's affirmative consent, the goods,
wares, merchandise, or products shall for all purposes be deemed an unconditional gift to the
consumer, who may use or dispose of the same in any manner such consumer sees fit without any
obligation whatsoever on the consumer's part to the business."

**NY GBL §527-a(1)(d) (parity):** cancellation must be available "at any time using a simple
cancellation mechanism that is as easy to use as the mechanism that the consumer used to
provide consent and that is through the same medium that the consumer used to provide consent."

**OR ORS 646A.295(5) (gift provision — Oregon has one too):** "In the event a person sends
goods, wares, merchandise or products to a consumer under a continuous service agreement or
pursuant to an automatic renewal of a purchase without first obtaining the consumer's
affirmative consent as required in subsection (1) of this section, the goods, wares,
merchandise or products shall for all purposes be deemed an unconditional gift to the consumer
who may use or dispose of them in any manner the consumer sees fit without any obligation to
the person..."

**FL §501.165(2)(f) (the entire remedy) — official ×2:** "A violation of this subsection
renders the automatic renewal provision void and unenforceable."
**FL §501.165(2)(d) (parity, added ch.2022-169):** "A seller that enters into or renews any
service contract with a consumer which includes an automatic renewal provision must allow the
consumer to cancel the service contract in the same manner, and by the same means, as the
consumer manifested his or her acceptance of the service contract."

**GA §10-1-399(b) (THE 30-DAY DEMAND GATE — a GA letter must satisfy this exactly) — verified
across FindLaw + ga.elaws.us + orchestrator re-read:** "At least 30 days prior to the filing
of any such action, a written demand for relief, identifying the claimant and reasonably
describing the unfair or deceptive act or practice relied upon and the injury suffered, shall
be delivered to any prospective respondent." A written tender of settlement within the 30 days,
if rejected, can limit recovery "to the relief tendered if the court finds that the relief
tendered was reasonable in relation to the injury actually suffered."
**GA §10-1-399(c):** "Subject to subsection (b) of this Code section, a court shall award
three times actual damages for an intentional violation." §10-1-399(d): fees for the injured
person, but denied for time "incurred after the rejection of a reasonable written offer of
settlement."
**GA §10-1-439.13 (the preclusion clause — NEVER cite Part 8 as the violated duty in a
private letter):** "There shall be no private right of action for a violation of this part,
nor shall a violation of this part serve as the basis for a private right of action under any
other provision of law."
**GA §13-12-5 (contract-defense remedy):** "A violation of this chapter renders the automatic
renewal provision of a contract void and unenforceable."

**HI §481-9.5(i) (scienter-gated per-se hook) — official PDF direct read:** "Any person who
knowingly violates this section or who knowingly fails to cancel an automatic renewal contract
or a continuous service contract upon consumer request shall be deemed to have engaged in an
unfair method of competition and unfair or deceptive act or practice in the conduct of any
trade or commerce within the meaning of section 480-2."

**HI §480-13(b)(1) (the $1,000-minimum question — PINNED, official PDF direct read):** "Any
consumer who is injured by any unfair or deceptive act or practice forbidden or declared
unlawful by section 480-2: (1) May sue for damages sustained by the consumer, and, if the
judgment is for the plaintiff, the plaintiff shall be awarded a sum not less than $1,000 or
threefold damages by the plaintiff sustained, whichever sum is the greater, and reasonable
attorney's fees together with the costs of suit; provided that where the plaintiff is an
elder, the plaintiff, in the alternative, may be awarded a sum not less than $5,000 or
threefold any damages sustained by the plaintiff, whichever sum is the greater..." (The
$1,000 minimum does NOT apply in class/de facto class actions, §480-13(c)(1). Official case
notes: fees are mandatory and count toward the "greater amount"; no punitive damages under
this chapter; §480-13 creates the private right of action for §480-2 violations.)

**DE §2732 (what counts as a "deceptive practice" — the scienter gate) — delcode direct:**
"In a contract for the sale or lease of merchandise to a consumer, a person engages in a
deceptive practice when that person knowingly or recklessly does any of the following: …
(3) Fails to comply with § 2734 or § 2735 of this title. (4) With respect to a contract that
automatically renews and without regard to the duration of such renewal period, fails to
provide a cost-effective, timely, and easy to use mechanism for cancellation. A consumer who
enters into a contract online shall be permitted to cancel the contract online."

**DE §2735(a)+(c) (the consumer remedy) — delcode direct:** "(a) Any person who engages in a
deceptive practice governed by this subchapter shall be liable to a consumer in an amount
equal to treble the amount of actual damages proved, plus reasonable attorneys' fees." /
"(c) With respect to any contract containing an automatic renewal provision that is renewed
in violation of § 2734 of this title, such contract is voidable by the consumer. … (2) A
consumer who voids a contract pursuant to this subsection is not liable for any costs, fees,
or expenses associated with the contract that accrue after the date on which the consumer
voided the contract. The seller may retain a prorated fraction of any prepaid fees or costs
based on the time since the renewal was executed and the time remaining in the renewal
period. The seller shall refund any remaining prepaid fees or costs to the consumer within
30 days."

**DE §2737(a) (pre-suit notice-and-cure gate — the letter IS this step) — delcode direct:**
"(1) Before bringing an action with respect to the automatic renewal of any contract
containing an automatic renewal provision that is renewed in violation of § 2734 of this
title, the consumer must provide the seller with notice of the violation and a request to
cancel the extension of the contract. … (2) An action may not be initiated under this chapter
by the consumer against the seller for the cured violation of § 2734 of this title if, within
30 days of the consumer sending the notice …, the seller does all of the following: a. Cures
the violation. b. Provides the consumer with a written statement that the alleged violation
has been cured and that no further violations of that kind will occur and sends a copy of
such statement to the Director of Consumer Protection of the Department of Justice."

**CA remedy ceiling — Mayron v. Google LLC (2020) 54 Cal.App.5th 566 (Sept. 11, 2020):**
"We conclude there is no private right of action for violation of the automatic renewal law."
Route = UCL §17200 unlawful prong; §17204 standing requires "injury in fact and has lost money
or property as a result of the unfair competition" (causation between the ARL violation and
the charge). Restitution-shaped relief, not statutory damages.

## 4. HONEST-NO — where we should NOT sell a letter (D10)

- **Colorado**: §6-1-732(6) exclusive AG/DA enforcement; violation deliberately excluded from
  the §6-1-105 deceptive-practices list, so no §6-1-113 private action. A letter can only
  threaten an AG/DA complaint. Sell (if anything) a FREE "complaint-ready summary," not a
  demand letter.
- **Washington**: no ARL exists (phantom "RCW 19.394"). Generic CPA only — too diffuse for a
  citation-locked letter. Honest-no.
- **Illinois**: sellable only as "refund demand," never as damages: the Sec. 10(c) safe harbor
  (mere "error" + refund) extinguishes both ACRA and Consumer Fraud Act liability, and CFA
  needs proven actual damage with no statutory minimum.
- **New York §527-a standing alone**: AG-only with $100-$1,000 penalties and a bona-fide-error
  safe harbor. Only letter-worthy because the §527-a(2) gift provision is self-executing and
  GBL §349(h) supplies a $50-floor private action for deceptive conduct.
- **Florida**: §501.165(2)(f)'s ONLY remedy is that the renewal provision is "void and
  unenforceable" — no PRA, no damages, no fees, no FDUTPA per-se hook (Part I of ch. 501,
  never cross-references Part II), plus a (2)(c) procedures+error+refund safe harbor and a
  wide exemption list. Sell (if anything) a void-provision refund demand ("charges billed
  under a void provision are not owed — refund the unearned portion"), priced as a refund
  letter, never a damages letter. Service contracts only.
- **Georgia (per-se route)**: the modern 2024 Act (§§10-1-439.5–.13) is AG-only AND carries
  an express preclusion clause barring its use "as the basis for a private right of action
  under any other provision of law" — a private letter citing Part 8 as the violated duty
  hands the seller a clean rebuttal. §13-12-5 is a contract defense (renewal void), not a
  damages claim. The only private damages route is the GENERIC FBPA deception theory
  (§10-1-393(a)) + §10-1-399 — treble only for intentional violations, and gated by the
  §10-1-399(b) 30-day written demand. Deception characterization is a [FLAG], never
  deterministic. Treat GA as weak: refund demand + optional FBPA-framed §10-1-399(b) demand
  where the facts genuinely support deception.
- **Everything federal**: ROSCA has no private right; FTC Section 5 has no private right; the
  2024 rule is vacated. Federal law is context, never the claim.
- **Any exempt-entity match** (see CATCHES): banks/credit unions, insurers, utilities/
  PSC-FCC-FERC-regulated sellers, alarm companies, service contracts — decline, in every
  verified state.

## 5. CATCHES (engine traps found in the primary text)

1. **Effective-date gates (D2).** CA's strongest duties (click-to-cancel button, annual
   reminder, consent retention) bind only contracts "entered into, amended, or extended … on
   or after July 1, 2025" (§17602(j)). VA's parity regime is effective July 1, 2026. DC
   applies only to renewals on/after Mar. 13, 2019 (§28A-221). IL Sec. 10 duties were phased
   in 1/1/2022 → 1/1/2024 → 1/1/2025 (P.A. 102-517, 103-70, 103-919). Phase-2 additions:
   DE's subchapter applies from Jan. 1, 2022 (§2737(c) verbatim); GA's Part 8 is effective
   Jan. 1, 2024 (AG-only anyway); VT from July 1, 2019; HI from 2011 (2015 amendments);
   OR from 2011; FL's parity duty (2)(d) was added by ch. 2022-169 — its exact
   applicability date to pre-2022 contracts is [UNVERIFIED — pull ch. 2022-169 effective/
   applicability section before any FL parity claim]. The engine MUST take sign-up date +
   latest renewal date and select the rule version in force — a 2024-signed CA subscription
   never modified since gets pre-AB 2863 law.
2. **Safe harbors flip the letter's theory.** CA §17604 (good-faith compliance), IL Sec. 10(c)
   (error + refund kills all liability), DC §28A-203(d) (procedures + good-faith mistake +
   refund), NY §527-a(3) (bona fide error), FL §501.165(2)(c) (procedures + error + refund of
   unearned portion), DE §2734(c) (same shape as FL), DE §2737(a) (30-day notice-and-cure —
   the demand letter is itself the statutory notice, and a full cure ends the claim), GA
   §10-1-399(b) (reasonable written tender caps recovery; §10-1-399(d) cuts fees after a
   reasonable offer). VT and OR have NO statutory safe harbor. Correct letter shape: demand
   the refund AS the safe-harbor/cure condition ("your only path out of treble exposure is
   the full refund the statute's own safe harbor requires").
3. **Contract-type/term thresholds.** Reminder duties trigger only above term floors: DC ≥12-mo
   initial term renewing ≥1 mo; IL ≥12-mo renewing >1 mo; VA renewal >30 days extending >12 mo;
   CA (b)(2) ≥1-yr initial term; FL/GA(13-12-3)/HI ≥12-mo term renewing >1 mo (30-60 days);
   DE renewal >1 mo causing effect >12 mo after initiation (30-60 days, EVERY extension);
   VT 30-60 days but the whole statute only reaches initial terms ≥1 yr renewing >1 mo —
   the single biggest VT gate (monthly subscriptions are categorically outside §2454a).
   OR has NO reminder duty at all — never claim one in an OR letter. VA's "automatic renewal"
   definition itself requires a renewal term >1 month — month-to-month plans must be pleaded
   as "continuous service." CO's 25-40-day reminder covers every renewal (aggregation rule)
   but is AG-only. Trial-notice duties have their own floors: CA >31 days, IL ≥15 days,
   DC ≥1 mo, VA >30 days; OR/HI trial duties are disclosure-shaped (cancel-before-payment),
   not windowed. FL and GA ch. 13-12 cover written SERVICE contracts only — goods
   subscriptions fall outside both.
4. **B2C scoping is inconsistent.** CO: individuals, personal/family/household only. IL: no
   B2B (Sec. 20(c)) AND "contract" = written agreement. DC "consumer" is unusually broad (any
   purchaser/lessee). VA uniquely covers small businesses (≤250 employees / ≤$10M receipts) —
   but whether a small business can claim VCPA §59.1-204 damages is [UNVERIFIED — definitional
   fight over "consumer transaction"].
5. **Exempt-entity lists are long and load-bearing.** Common across CA §17605 / NY §527-a(4) /
   DC §28A-204 / CO §6-1-732(5) / IL Sec. 20 / VA §59.1-207.48: banks and credit unions +
   subsidiaries/affiliates, insurers, PUC/PSC/FCC/FERC-regulated services, alarm companies,
   service contracts. CO uniquely exempts air carriers. The intake MUST classify the seller
   before any letter is offered.
6. **The gift provision only fits GOODS.** CA §17603 / NY §527-a(2) / VA §59.1-207.47 /
   OR 646A.295(5) all say "goods, wares, merchandise, or products" — a streaming/SaaS
   subscription gets no gift theory; the lever there is parity/notice violations + refund
   demand (UCL restitution in CA, §349 in NY, VCPA in VA, CPPA in DC, 646.638 in OR).
6a. **Scienter gates (NEW — phase 2).** Four of the six new states condition the damages
   lever on state of mind: HI §481-9.5(i) "knowingly violates … or knowingly fails to
   cancel"; DE §2732 "knowingly or recklessly"; OR 646.638(1) "willful use or employment";
   GA §10-1-399(c) treble only for "intentional violation". Letters must PLEAD FACTS
   supporting scienter ([FLAG], never deterministic) — and the strongest scienter fact is a
   documented, ignored cancellation request (HI's second prong is nearly self-proving on
   that fact pattern). VT is the exception: §2454a(c) is strict per-se with no scienter
   word — but see the §2461(b) "false or fraudulent" textual-breadth caveat in §3.
6b. **VT double-opt-in is checklist gold.** §2454a(a)(1)-(3) is binary and auditable from
   the sign-up UI alone (bold-face terms + separate opt-in action + 30-60-day notice).
   Any VT contract in scope that lacks the SEPARATE renewal opt-in is non-compliant on its
   face — the cleanest deterministic check in the whole corpus.
7. **VA cure-offer trap.** VCPA §59.1-204(C)/(A): a seller's timely cure offer strips
   post-offer attorney fees, and acceptance bars further claims on the same facts. Letters
   must price the demand knowing a fast refund offer ends the leverage.
8. **Mirror rot is real (D3).** Cornell's CFR still shows the vacated 2024 rule as current.
   CO spot-check is now CLOSED (official CRS 2024 PDF via Wayback id_ snapshot confirmed the
   mirror text verbatim — see CO row). Still mirror-dependent: OR (oregonlegislature.gov
   refuses egress; dual-mirror verbatim match) and GA (O.C.G.A. is Lexis-gated; FindLaw +
   ga.elaws.us match, code-year ~2024). Every reference-data file must carry its fetch
   source + date. Wrong-cite corrections found this pass: "ORS 646A.294" does not exist;
   GA's ARL is NOT in §10-1-393(b) (verified negative — no ARL paragraph in the FBPA list);
   DE's ARL is ch. 27 subch. IV, not ch. 25; NY's enactment chapter is L.2020 c.267, not
   c.219; HI's threshold structure is term-tiered (>1-mo / ≥12-mo), not "60-day".
9. **DC annotation ambiguity.** §§28A-202/-204 pages still carry stale "not implemented"
   funding notes while §28A-203's note says the condition was removed (L.23-16 §3022). The
   codified §28A-221 applicability text supports the chapter being in force. Flagged
   [UNVERIFIED] — re-confirm before shipping DC letters.
