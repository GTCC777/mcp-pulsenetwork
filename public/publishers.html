Who we are

The Aslan Group LLC operates PulseNetwork, a network of 68 specialized intelligence properties — covering travel, personal finance, health & wellness, climate & weather, sports analysis, crypto/token safety, careers, real estate, insurance, and more. Across the network we publish 733 distinct pieces of structured content (we call them "endpoints" internally — think of each one as a recurring column or briefing format within a property).

What makes PulseNetwork unusual as a publisher is distribution: instead of a reader opening a browser tab, our content is requested and read by an AI assistant on a person's behalf — inside Claude, inside an autonomous shopping or research agent, or through a direct API integration. The person still receives the recommendation; they just receive it inside their AI assistant's answer rather than on a web page they scrolled to. Every property still has a human-readable home page (linked below) so anyone — including you, reviewing this application — can see exactly what a given property covers and how it's presented.

How recommendations surface

Each property returns a researched, structured answer to a specific question — a destination weather brief, a deal roundup, a token safety scan, a financial snapshot — and where a merchant recommendation is genuinely relevant to that answer, we place the link ourselves, server-side, next to the content it supports. There is no ad network in the loop and no ad auction deciding placement; an editor (in our case, code we wrote and maintain) decides where a link belongs based on the content, the same way a human editor would decide where to place a product mention in an article.

Audience & distribution

We're honest that this is a young, fast-growing network, not a legacy publisher with a decade of traffic history — and we'd rather tell you exactly how young than round up.

How readers reach us

  • Direct AI-assistant integrations — Claude and the broader MCP (Model Context Protocol) ecosystem
  • Agent marketplaces and discovery indexes: CDP Bazaar (Coinbase), x402scan, and Agentic.market — 71 of our properties are indexed there today, spanning 697 of our content endpoints
  • Direct API consumers — developers and agent-builders who integrate a property straight into their own product

Real, verifiable numbers

The network launched in 2026. In the first ~2.5 days of tracking external (non-us) demand — July 1–3, 2026 — 44 distinct external customers made 140 paid calls across 23 of our 68 properties. That's a small, early, but real and growing base of paying readers, not a projection.

We don't have — and won't fabricate — legacy pageview or subscriber numbers. What we can show you is live: every property publishes its own openapi.json and documentation, and SignalPulse publishes an ongoing, public accuracy track record.

Brand safety & compliance

Family-safe, no gambling operations

SignalPulse and our other sports properties publish analysis and commentary only — we never operate a sportsbook, take a wager, or hold customer funds against an outcome. Nothing in the network is age-restricted, adult, or gambling-operational content.

Honest-calibration track record

Where we publish predictions (sports/market analysis), we track and publish our own accuracy history publicly — including the misses — rather than curating only the wins. See SignalPulse's public track record ↗.

Disclosure by default

Every response that carries an affiliate/merchant link includes a disclosure field inline, in the response itself — not only on a separate legal page. See our affiliate disclosure below.

No incentivized or manufactured traffic

We do not run incentivized-click programs, cookie-stuffing, or any form of forced/hidden affiliate redirection. We do not bid on merchant trademark terms in paid search. Links are placed once, server-side, next to genuinely relevant content — never injected retroactively into someone else's traffic.

Affiliate disclosure

PulseNetwork (operated by The Aslan Group LLC) may earn a commission when you use a merchant link included in one of our responses or pages.

Our recommendations are generated independently of any commission — a destination, product, or service is suggested because it's relevant to the question asked, using the same research and logic whether or not an affiliate relationship exists for that merchant. Commission does not influence which merchant is recommended or how prominently. This disclosure also lives at its own address for easy reference: pulse.theaslangroupllc.com/affiliate-disclosure.

FAQ for reviewers

Why don't I see a traditional blog or article pages?

Because most of our readers are AI assistants requesting an answer on a person's behalf, not a browser loading a page to scroll through. Each property still has a human-readable home page and full documentation (linked from every property page and from the hub catalog) — this page and those home pages are the human-readable layer of a network whose primary distribution channel is the AI-assistant ecosystem.

Is this an API product, or a media property?

Both, and intentionally so — the same way a newspaper is both "a printing operation" and "a media property." The delivery mechanism is an API (structured, per-question, paid per call); what it delivers is editorial content: researched recommendations, written and reviewed for a specific reader question, with merchant links placed the way an editor would place them.

Who owns and operates PulseNetwork?

The Aslan Group LLC, a U.S. company. Contact: info@theaslangroupllc.com.

How is this different from a normal affiliate site?

Mechanically it's the same relationship — we place tracked merchant links next to relevant content and disclose that we may earn a commission. The difference is distribution: instead of a person clicking through a search result to a page, an AI assistant requests the content on the person's behalf, and the same disclosed link travels with the answer.

Can I verify the properties are real and live?

Yes — every property link on this page resolves to a live site with its own documentation and (where applicable) a free sample endpoint. The hub catalog at pulse.theaslangroupllc.com/#catalog lists all 68 with links to each one's live openapi.json.
