The deal in one paragraph

You buy prepaid report credits from The Aslan Group LLC ("Aslan") at wholesale, self-serve. You resell reports to your own customers — under your own brand if you like, at your own price, through your own payment setup. Aslan is not a party to your sales and takes no cut of them. Once you've bought credits, they're yours: they never expire, are never repriced, and your key keeps working until the balance runs out.

What you're buying

Each credit runs one report (per the posted per-endpoint credit costs). Reports are tax and on-chain intelligence, not tax, legal, or financial advice. Every report says this on its face, states exactly what it did and didn't cover, and carries machine-verified citations so a licensed professional can check every claim against the primary source. That professional review step is the product's design, not fine print: reports are decision-support for a professional to verify — they are not filings, opinions, or advice.

The three rules

  1. Keep the report honest. White-label freely — your brand, your layout, your delivery. But the findings, citations, verification statements, coverage disclosures, and disclaimers stay intact and visible. They're the product's integrity (and your liability shield too).
  2. Pass the professional-review posture through. However you present reports to your customers, make clear that a licensed tax or financial professional should review the information for accuracy before anyone relies on it.
  3. Market it truthfully. Don't call it tax advice, don't promise outcomes, don't claim any government endorsement, and only run reports on wallet addresses you or your customer may lawfully review.
