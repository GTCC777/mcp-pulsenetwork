The Unbundled Index

Most expensive data subscriptions sell curation of public facts, not the facts themselves. We decomposed 22 gated data products — from $39/mo tools to a $212,000/yr citation index — into the free public data underneath, what each subscription genuinely adds, and whether a per-call rebuild is viable. Verdicts: 13 rebuildable from free public data, 4 partially, 4 honestly not (their moat is real).

The discriminating test: if a product's moat is curation of public facts, it can be rebuilt and served per-call; if its moat is data that never touches a public record (private accident histories, canvassed OTC prices, voluntary disclosure networks), it cannot — and we say so.
